Study case

Let's get to serious business but not too much

Let's take an example of an attack of a Pokémon on another, to see the full extand of what is behind the scenes. Suppose there is a Charizard that wants to use the move Flamethrower on a Mega-Venusaur. Let's assume for simplicity that both Pokémons are at level 100 (the maximum).

Each pokémon has base stats defining how strong it is in different domains. HP (Hit Points) are the life a Pokémon has. Attack and Special Attack defines the power of the physical or special moves the Pokémon uses. Defense and Special Defense is for the resistance a Pokémon has against such attacks. And finally, Speed is how fast a Pokémon can attack.

Using thoses stats, we can now look at the power of Flamethrower. Each move has a power defining the damages you can do with such an attack. Flamethrower has a power of 90, which is a pretty strong. But the effective power of Charizard using Flamethrower on Mega-Venusaur is 270! The cause of that is two things that you may or may not know.

Type advantage
This is one of the basis of Pokémon. Each one of them has one or two types and some types are stronger than others. Moves also have a type. Flamethrower is obviously fire type. Mega-Venusaur has grass type and poison type. Since fire is super effective against grass, the power is doubled.
STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus)
This one is a little less known. If a Pokémon uses a move that is the same type it has, it's more effective. Since both Charizard and Flamethower have fire type, the power's move is 1.5 times more effective

Now let's get to the real deal. A Mega-Venusaur at level 100 has around 300 hit points. The damages the attack deals simply remove as much HP on the target. If we calculate the damages of this move using the stats that we have, we get 194 damage. That is roughly two-thirds of Mega-Venusaur's hit points, which is great, considering that it's special-defense is pretty high...

But what if I tell you that in the right conditions, we could go up to 2670 damages and on other conditions, it will only do as much as 6 damages ? Let's see how it works.

Effective Stat

I didn't told you everything about stats. There is a formula for that too. Beware, it can be scary.

Ok, that's not so scary. But you can see that there is more than just the base stat, let's get to them all.

Base
This is the value I showed you earlier. This value has the greatest influence on the effective stat. For Charizard, it's 107 but let's take 100 to make it simple.
IV ( Individual Value)
The Individual Value is a number between 0 and 31 that is represented in each stat. The IV of each Pokémon - even of the same species - can be different. It's like it's body characteristics. Some Charizards are faster than other since their birth and the IV is there to represent those differences. Let's suppose our Charizard has 31 in Special Attack.
EV ( Effort Value)
Effort Value is another number, between 0 and 255 for each stat. Unlike IVs, EVs are at 0 when they are born. They represent the training that a Pokémon can have for a particular stat. For every 4 EVs, a Pokémon level 100 has 1 more point in the effective stat. They gain those EVs by defeating other Pokémons and the stat they affect depends on the opponent. For instance, defeating a Charizard rewards 3 EVs in Special Attack. Let's suppose our Charizard has maxed EVs in Special Attack, that's 63 more on the effective stat.
Nature
The nature of a Pokémon also influences his stats. Usually, a nature increases slightly a stat and decreases another by 10%. If we suppose our Charizard has a Rash nature, it makes him stronger in Special Attack.

Modifiers

So many of them

There is a lot of things that can make our attack so much stronger. Let's get crazy on offense.
STAB and Type
We already talked about those, that makes our attack stronger by 1.5 and 2 respectively
Harsh sun
Some moves can alter the weather and those can make attacks stronger. In this case, the move Sunny Day can make a harsh sun weather, and that results in our move being 1.5 more effective.
Helping Hand
Another move that can help is Helping Hand (pun not intended). This move can be used by a Pokémon that is next to our Charizard (yes, battle with multiple Pokémons is a thing) and it allows his next attack to be 1.5 times more powerful. That's the power of friendship !
Blaze
Each Pokémon has a special talent called Abilities that can alter a bunch of things in the game. In this case, Blaze is a simple ability that makes fire moves 1.5 times more effective if it's health is below one third. Let's assume our Charizard got hit by his opponent, evil Mega-Venusaur and his health is currently below that point.
Life Orb
In the Pokémon world, there is also a lot of items that can enhance our Pokémons' abilities if they hold them. Life Orb is a simple item. The Pokémon takes 10% damages for each attack he makes but it's more effective by ~1.3 (5324/4096 exactly, Arceus knows why).

With all that in mind, we are now all set to calculate the damage of the move with the damage formula :

Level is 100
Power is 90
A for attack (special attack in our case) is 657.8
D for special defense (Mega-Venusaur) is 245 (120 * 2 + 5)
Modifier is 1.5 * 2 * 1.5 * 1.5 * 1.5 * 5324/4096

With that, we come to a result of 2671 damages, or more than 8 times Mega-Venusaur's HP!

You know how it works, let's do the reverse

Instead of giving all we have in offense, let's see in the defensive side. We can greatly affect the outcome by giving everything we got to Mega-Venusaur. Full EVs and IVs ? Let's go for it. A good nature and a stage 2 special defense ? It gets that. An assault Vest giving him a 1.5 boost in defense ? Just bought it.

With all that, let's add the Thick Fat ability, a little rain instead of a harsh sun and a Light Screen (that's a move, not a lotion). For each one of those modifiers, we divide the final damages by 2! STAB and types remain the same.

With that, we come to a result of 6 damages. Mega-Venusaur will eventually be beaten after more than 50 attacks.

What is fun is that if we give both all that we have (let's make it Sunny to even things out) we get to 148 damages, that just not enough to take it down in two attacks.

Conclusion

Aren't you amazed yet ?

Just with this example, you can see that there is more depth in a Pokémon battle. We just scratched the surface of how deep and complex it can get. For instance, we haven't talked about criticals or special moves. There is some status effects that can alter a Pokémon's stats and behaviour. There is also an evasion/precision mechanic that alters if a move does it or not. And there is many abilities, moves or items that can alter all that. And that's only if we are talking about battle!

And there is a lot of variety in the kind of Pokémons we can have. Stats differ greatly between each one of them. But it's also a world with creatures that follow certain rules. Just look at the graph below. It shows the average value of each stat for each type of Pokémon. From that, you can recognize some patterns. For instance, if you look at the Bug type, you see that the average values are pretty low. That's beacause many of the Bug Pokémons are early game Pokémons that evolves quickly but are not as strong as others. That's the opposite with Dragon Pokémons which can be found at the end game.

Other things you can gather. Flying Pokémons are faster than the others types and Steel Pokémons have the highest Defense by far. Fighting Pokémons uses mainly contact and physical moves, so their Attack is very high compared to their Special Attack that is very low. You can see that it's the opposite with Psychic Pokémons.

You can put your mouse over a stat to get it's value and hide other stats a little. You can also click on the legend on the right to completely hide every stat but one. Click on another to change, or on the same one to get them all back.
